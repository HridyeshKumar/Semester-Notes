The GLD kernel is a concept used in the field of computational physics, specifically in the context of Generalized Langevin Dynamics (GLD). It is used to model the effect of an implicit solvent with a temporally non-local dissipative force and a colored Gaussian random force, consistent with the Fluctuation-Dissipation Theorem.

The drift matrix couples the physical dynamics with that of the additional degrees of freedom, making it possible to obtain a history-dependent noise and friction kernel.
